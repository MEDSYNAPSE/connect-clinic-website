Section 1

Before you book

Make sure Connect Clinic is the right service for your needs before you begin.

We can help with

  • ✓ Repeat prescriptions for stable, established conditions
  • ✓ Medical certificates for illness or injury
  • ✓ General health concerns and symptom review
  • ✓ Weight management and Wegovy (semaglutide) assessment
  • ✓ Dietician consultation (telehealth referral or direct booking)
  • ✓ Second opinions and condition reviews
  • ✓ Acute illness review (cold, flu, respiratory, UTI, skin infections)

Not suitable for telehealth

  • ! Life-threatening emergencies — call 111 immediately (chest pain, stroke, difficulty breathing, major trauma)
  • ! Mental health crisis — call or text 1737 (free, 24/7), or call 111
  • ! Controlled drug prescriptions — opioids (e.g. morphine, oxycodone, codeine), benzodiazepines (e.g. diazepam, clonazepam), methylphenidate, dexamfetamine — an in-person GP assessment is required by NZ law
  • ! Conditions requiring physical examination — abdominal pain (possible surgical), ear infections in children under 2, suspected fractures
  • ! New or complex mental health presentations — please contact your local mental health service or enroll with a community GP

What to have ready

■

NHI number

Find it on any NZ hospital letter or my health record. Can't find it? Call 0800 855 066.

■

Current medication list

Include name, dose, and frequency (e.g. metformin 500 mg twice daily).

■

Known drug allergies

Include the reaction type if known (e.g. penicillin — anaphylaxis).

■

Recent test results

Blood tests, imaging reports (X-ray, CT, MRI), or specialist letters if relevant to your concern.

■

Preferred pharmacy

Name and address of the pharmacy where you'd like your prescription sent.

■

Photo ID (optional)

Dr Katoa may ask to verify your identity via a brief ID check at the start of your consult.

Section 2

How to book

The entire process takes just a few minutes online — no phone calls required.

1

Complete the eligibility check

Visit connectclinic.co.nz and complete the short eligibility check to confirm your request is suitable for telehealth. This takes about 2 minutes.

2

Fill in your clinical history

Provide your personal details and clinical background — current medications, known drug allergies, relevant family history, and any recent hospital admissions or specialist visits. This information helps Dr Katoa prepare for your consultation.

3

Submit your request

Submit your booking request. You will receive a booking reference by email confirming your request has been received. Please retain this email.

4

Complete payment

Your appointment is confirmed once payment is completed via Stripe secure checkout. Stripe is a PCI-DSS Level 1 compliant payment provider. Connect Clinic does not store your card details.

5

Appointment confirmation

Dr Katoa will review your request and confirm your appointment time by email — typically within a few hours during business hours (Monday–Thursday, 9 am–5 pm NZST).

6

Join your Zoom consultation

At your appointment time, join via the Zoom link provided in your confirmation email. No download is required — Zoom works directly in any modern web browser. Ensure you're in a private, well-lit location with a stable internet connection.

Section 3

During your consult

What to expect when you meet with Dr Katoa via Zoom.

Platform

Zoom video call. No download required — works in any browser. Click the link in your confirmation email to join. You may be asked to allow camera and microphone access.

Identity verification

Dr Katoa will verify your identity at the start of the consult, typically by asking you to confirm your full name, date of birth, and NHI number. You may be asked to briefly show photo ID.

Confidentiality

Your consultation is fully confidential and conducted under the NZ Health Information Privacy Code 2020. Please ensure you are in a private location where you feel comfortable speaking freely.

Duration

Consultations typically last 10–15 minutes. Complex reviews or Wegovy assessments may take up to 20–25 minutes. Please be available a few minutes before your scheduled time.

Telehealth limitations

No physical examination is performed — this is a telehealth assessment only. Dr Katoa will advise if an in-person examination is clinically necessary and can provide an onward referral.

After the call

You will receive your prescription, medical certificate, or referral letter by email following the consultation — typically within 1–2 hours.

Have your documents ready during the call

Keep your medication list, any relevant test results, and recent correspondence from specialists or hospitals visible or accessible on your device. This allows the consultation to run efficiently and accurately.

Section 4

Prescriptions

Connect Clinic issues electronic prescriptions (e-prescriptions) in accordance with NZ prescribing regulations and the NZ Formulary (NZF).

Electronic prescriptions (e-Rx)

Prescriptions are sent electronically (via the NZ E-Prescribing system) directly to your nominated pharmacy. You do not need a paper script — simply present your NHI number at the pharmacy counter.

Funded (Pharmac) medications

Medications subsidised by Pharmac are dispensed at the standard NZ co-payment rate of $5.00 per item. Patients who hold a Community Services Card or High Use Health Card may qualify for free dispensing. Confirm eligibility with your pharmacist.

Prescription validity

A standard prescription is valid for 3 months from the date of issue, unless otherwise directed on the prescription. Please fill your prescription promptly — expiry dates cannot be extended.

Repeat prescriptions

Suitable for patients with a stable, established diagnosis and an existing prescription history. Examples include antihypertensives, lipid-lowering agents (e.g. atorvastatin), oral hypoglycaemics (e.g. metformin), thyroid replacement therapy (e.g. levothyroxine), and oral contraceptives.

Controlled drugs cannot be prescribed via telehealth

Under the NZ Misuse of Drugs Act 1975 and associated regulations, the following drug classes require an in-person clinical assessment before prescribing:

  • •Opioid analgesics — e.g. morphine, oxycodone, codeine (Class B/C controlled drugs)
  • •Benzodiazepines — e.g. diazepam, lorazepam, clonazepam, temazepam (Class C)
  • •Stimulants / ADHD medications — methylphenidate (Ritalin), dexamfetamine (Class B)
  • •Zopiclone — note: while not a benzodiazepine, regular prescribing of zopiclone via telehealth is not appropriate without established care

Please contact your regular enrolled GP practice or a community pharmacy for guidance on accessing these medications.

Section 5

Medical certificates

Certificates are issued following a telehealth clinical assessment and are legally valid NZ medical documents.

Legally recognised

Certificates are accepted by employers, Work and Income NZ (WINZ), ACC, and educational institutions across New Zealand.

Delivered by email

Your certificate is emailed directly to you as a PDF document within 1–2 hours of your consultation. You can forward it directly to your employer or institution.

ACC injury documentation

If your consultation relates to an ACC-covered injury, please mention this when booking. Dr Katoa can issue ACC documentation (ACC45 lodgement) where clinically appropriate during a telehealth assessment.

No backdated certificates

Dr Katoa is unable to issue backdated medical certificates. Certificates are issued from the date of the clinical assessment only. This is consistent with MCNZ guidance on ethical prescribing and certification practice.

Section 6

After your consult

What happens after your appointment and how to follow up.

Documents emailed within 1–2 hours

Your prescription, medical certificate, or referral letter will be sent to the email address provided at booking. If you haven't received anything after 2 hours, check your spam or junk folder first, then contact us at connectclinic@outlook.com.

Follow-up consultations

If your condition changes or worsens after your consultation, please book a follow-up consult through the website. Do not wait — if your symptoms are urgent, contact Healthline (0800 611 116) or call 111 in an emergency.

Safety netting

As part of good clinical practice, Dr Katoa will provide safety-netting advice at the end of each consultation — including specific symptoms or warning signs that should prompt you to seek urgent care. Please take note of this advice before ending the call.

GP records and continuity of care

Connect Clinic maintains clinical records for all consultations. If you have a regular enrolled GP, you are welcome to request a copy of your consultation notes to share with them. Email connectclinic@outlook.com to request a records transfer.

Section 8

Privacy & confidentiality

Your health information is treated with the highest level of confidentiality in accordance with NZ law.

Health Information Privacy Code 2020

All health information collected is governed by the NZ Health Information Privacy Code 2020 (HIPC 2020), administered by the Office of the Privacy Commissioner. Your information is collected only for the purposes of your clinical care.

Secure storage

Clinical records are stored securely and are not shared with third parties without your explicit consent — except where required by NZ law (see below).

Mandatory reporting obligations

Dr Katoa has obligations under NZ law to disclose certain information without consent — including risk of serious harm to self or others, child protection concerns (Children, Young Persons and Their Families Act), and notifiable infectious diseases (Health Act 1956).

Accessing your records

You have the right to access your health information held by Connect Clinic. To request a copy of your records, email connectclinic@outlook.com. Requests will be responded to within 20 working days as per the HIPC 2020.

111

0800 611 116
Healthline

Free 24/7 health advice from registered nurses. Speak to a health professional when you're unsure what to do. Available all hours, every day.

0800 764 766
National Poisons Centre

24/7 expert advice for poisoning or overdose — including medications, household chemicals, plants, and insect stings. Free to call anywhere in NZ.

If you're uncertain whether your situation is an emergency, call Healthline 0800 611 116. A registered nurse will help you decide on the most appropriate course of action — whether that's an emergency service, urgent care clinic, or a telehealth consult.
