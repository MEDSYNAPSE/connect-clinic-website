# Connect Clinic Limited — NZ Funding Pipeline (FY2026)

Prepared 29 April 2026. Connect Clinic is a private NZ Ltd (Dr Francis Katoa, Tongan-NZ GP, FRNZCGP). Service: Pasifika and Māori-focused preventive telehealth via Elixir PMS (GP + Health Coach), mental wellbeing & lifestyle change focus.

**Critical structural note:** most NZ philanthropic funders explicitly require charitable purpose. To unlock the full pipeline, recommend either (a) registering a sister charitable trust **Connect Clinic Foundation** or (b) auspicing through a registered Pacific charity (Le Va, K'aute Pasifika, Pasifika Medical Association, Cause Collective, or a Pacific PHO partner) for Round 1 applications.

## The pitch hooks (stat-backed)

### Hook 1 — "The 5.5-year life expectancy gap is closable, and prevention is where it closes"
> Pacific peoples live **5.5 years less** than NZ Europeans (Te Mana Ola, MoH 2023). Type 2 diabetes prevalence in Pasifika is **~15%, three times the national rate** (BMC Health Services Research 2022). Connect Clinic targets the upstream lifestyle and mental-wellbeing drivers of this gap with culturally Pacific-led care, before patients hit the diabetes / CVD / suicide crisis end of the pipeline.

### Hook 2 — "GP + coach hybrid solves the 'diagnose-and-abandon' gap that drives Pacific equity outcomes"
> NZ's *Te Tumu Waiora* integrated primary mental health model showed health-coach-in-general-practice "**significantly increased access for three key groups not served well by traditional service approaches — young people, Māori and Pacific**" (Te Pou / ProCare evaluation). **35% of Pacific peoples 65+ live with multimorbidity** (vs 15% of Europeans, MoH 2024). A Pacific GP plus a Pacific Health Coach by telehealth gives whānau the diagnosis *and* the 6-month behaviour-change scaffold.

### Hook 3 — "Telehealth + Pacific cultural fluency reaches the patients who never make it to a clinic"
> Pacific peoples have higher unmet primary care need than any other ethnic group, driven by cost, transport, work hours, and shame around mental health (Health & Independence Report 2024). Telehealth via Elixir PMS, in Tongan/Samoan/Cook Islands Māori where needed, with after-hours coach contact, removes the structural barriers — not just the clinical ones.
