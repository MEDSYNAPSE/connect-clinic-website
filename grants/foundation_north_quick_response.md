## 2. Project summary

Talanoa Ola is a 12-week telehealth pilot delivered by Connect Clinic — a newly incorporated, Pacific GP-led practice (Companies Office registration 29 April 2026) — designed to close preventable health gaps for Pasifika and Māori whānau in Tāmaki Makaurau. Pacific peoples in Aotearoa live an average of **5.5 years less** than the total population (Te Mana Ola, 2023), are **three times more likely** to develop type 2 diabetes (BMC Public Health, 2022), and **35% of Pacific people aged 65+ live with multimorbidity** (Ministry of Health, 2024). These are not statistics our communities accept as inevitable. They are the consequence of a system that has not been built around how our people seek care, talk about wellbeing, or trust providers.

The pilot pairs a Pacific GP (Dr Francis Katoa, FRNZCGP) with a Pacific Health Coach (Warren Aseloka, joining May 2026) in a hybrid model adapted from the Te Tumu Waiora evidence base. Twenty-five Pasifika and Māori whānau will be enrolled for a structured 12-week programme combining clinical review, lifestyle change support, mental wellbeing coaching, and culturally grounded talanoa sessions delivered in English, Tongan, Samoan, and te reo Māori where requested. Care is coordinated through Elixir Practice Management System with whānau-friendly digital touchpoints.

Our request is for $25,000 to fund the pilot setup phase — the cultural design sprint, technology configuration, evaluation framework, and koha for whānau participants. We are seeking this funding under the auspice of [TBC partner], a registered charitable entity aligned with Foundation North's charitable-purpose requirements. Connect Clinic Limited is the operational delivery vehicle; the auspice partner will hold the funds and provide governance oversight in line with Foundation North's terms.

## 4. Target outcomes

Aligned to **Te Mana Ola — Pacific Health Strategy** priorities (equity, prevention, Pacific workforce, whānau-centred care) and **Whānau Ora outcomes** (whānau are self-managing, leading healthy lifestyles, participating fully in society, confidently participating in te ao Māori).

**Clinical outcomes (n=25 whānau, measured pre/post 12 weeks):**
- ≥70% of enrolled whānau complete the full 12-week programme.
- ≥50% of those with raised HbA1c at baseline show measurable improvement (≥5 mmol/mol drop).
- ≥60% report improvement on the WHO-5 Wellbeing Index.
- ≥80% of whānau have an active, agreed care plan in Elixir at week 12.

**Equity & cultural outcomes:**
- 100% of whānau report (via post-programme talanoa) that the service felt culturally safe and that they were "treated as whānau, not a patient ID".
- ≥3 Pacific languages actively used in consultations.
- 1 published case-study report co-authored with whānau participants (with consent).

**System outcomes:**
- A reusable, evaluated GP+coach Pacific care model documented and shareable with other Pacific providers, K'aute Pasifika, The Fono, and Te Whatu Ora Pacific Provider Development Fund (PPDF) commissioning teams.

## 5. Why Connect Clinic

Connect Clinic is led by **Dr Francis Katoa** — a Tongan-NZ GP, FRNZCGP, born and raised in Auckland, with whānau roots in Vava'u and Ha'apai. Francis has practised across multiple Auckland Pacific and Māori communities (including Pasifika Rise and The Fono context) and has spent the last two years building digital tools to improve Pacific access to primary care. Connect Clinic is the next step: a purpose-built, telehealth-first, Pacific-led GP practice.

What is different about us:

- **Pacific GP-led, not Pacific-adjacent.** The clinical voice and the strategic voice are the same Pacific voice. Decisions about care pathways, language, koha, and pacing are made by people who live the culture.
- **GP + coach hybrid grounded in Te Tumu Waiora evidence.** The model has demonstrated impact in Aotearoa primary care; we are adapting it for Pacific and Māori whānau with culturally tailored coaching and talanoa-based engagement.
- **Mature technology spine (Elixir PMS).** We are not building from scratch. Elixir gives us safe clinical records, recall registers, and reporting from day one. Telehealth removes transport and time-off-work barriers that disproportionately affect Pacific whānau.
- **Whānau-centred, not whānau-aimed.** Every touchpoint is designed with kaiawhina and community advisors before it goes live. We use talanoa methodology to build the programme *with* whānau, not *for* them.

## 7. Evaluation plan

We will use a mixed-methods, **whānau-centred evaluation** approach.

**Quantitative (Elixir PMS + REDCap):**
- Clinical: HbA1c, blood pressure, weight, waist circumference, smoking status, lipid profile (where indicated).
- Wellbeing: K10 and WHO-5 at week 0, week 6, week 12.
- Engagement: appointment attendance, coach contact frequency, care-plan completion.

**Qualitative (talanoa methodology):**
- Closing talanoa session with each whānau at week 12 (~30 min, recorded with consent, transcribed).
- Thematic analysis led by Dr Katoa with cultural review by Pacific community advisor.
- Quotes (de-identified, with consent) included in the final report to honour whānau voice.

**Reporting:**
- Mid-pilot progress report to Foundation North + auspice partner at week 6.
- Full evaluation report at week 14 — written in plain English with a one-page Pacific-language summary.
- A community talanoa event at week 16 to share findings back with whānau and partners.

## 8. Sustainability

The 12-week pilot is the proof point — not the destination. Three pathways carry the programme forward:

1. **Te Whatu Ora — Pacific Provider Development Fund (PPDF).** PPDF supports Pacific-led primary care infrastructure. The pilot's evaluation report will become the evidence base for a 12-month PPDF application in 2027 to fund a Pacific GP + coach service line at Connect Clinic.
2. **Tātou Whānau Ora commissioning (Te Pou Matakana / Pasifika Futures).** The whānau-centred model and outcomes framework align with Whānau Ora commissioning priorities. We will pursue a co-commissioned coaching contract for an additional cohort.
3. **Earned revenue — paid coaching tier.** For whānau who choose private coaching post-pilot, Connect Clinic will offer an affordable coaching package (subsidised for low-income whānau via cross-subsidy). This generates a small but durable revenue stream to keep the model viable between funding cycles.

The auspice partner will be invited to remain a strategic partner beyond the pilot, and IP from the cultural design sprint will be jointly held to enable other Pacific providers to adapt the model.

## 9. Anticipated challenges

We are honest about the risks, and we have planned for them.

**Engagement and dropout (Pacific telehealth).** Pacific whānau are sometimes wary of telehealth due to digital access, language preference, and prior experiences of impersonal "system" care. *Mitigation:* the first contact for every whānau is a kanohi-ki-te-kanohi or phone talanoa with the coach, in the whānau's preferred language, before any video appointment. Koha acknowledges the time whānau give, not "incentivises attendance".

**Digital access.** Some whānau will have data or device limitations. *Mitigation:* the programme works equally well via phone call, SMS, and audio-only — we do not require video. Coach-supplied data top-ups are budgeted within the koha line.

**Workforce capacity.** This is a small team. *Mitigation:* the cohort is intentionally capped at 25 whānau. Clinical supervision is provided by Dr Katoa, with peer supervision via PMA networks.

**Auspice arrangement timing.** The auspice MOU must be signed before drawdown. *Mitigation:* discussions with three potential partners are underway; a preferred partner will be confirmed before submission.

**Charitable-purpose scrutiny.** Foundation North will reasonably ask how a private company applies through an auspice. *Mitigation:* the charitable-purpose vehicle is the auspice partner. Connect Clinic is the delivery vehicle. All deliverables (the evaluation, the cultural resources, the model) are open for the partner and broader Pacific community to use.
