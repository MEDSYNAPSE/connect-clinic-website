## 2. Proposed working title

**Vā Ola: A Pacific-led GP and Health-Coach Telehealth Trial for Metabolic and Mental Wellbeing in Tāmaki Makaurau**

(Vā = the relational space; Ola = life, wellbeing. The vā between clinician, coach, and whānau is the active ingredient we are testing.)

---

## 3. The research question

> **Does a culturally-grounded, Pacific-led GP-plus-coach telehealth model improve preventive metabolic and mental wellbeing outcomes for Pacific whānau in Auckland over 12 months, compared to usual primary care?**

Co-primary outcomes: change in HbA1c, and change in Kessler-10 (or PHQ-9 where culturally preferred). We hypothesise a clinically meaningful improvement in both, sustained at 12 months, with a between-arm difference large enough to justify a national rollout business case to Te Whatu Ora and Te Aka Whai Ora.

---

## 4. Why this question, why now

The 5.5-year Pacific life-expectancy gap reported in Te Mana Ola (Ministry of Health, 2023) is not closing, and the upstream drivers are well-mapped: type 2 diabetes prevalence in Pasifika sits at roughly 15%, three times the national rate (BMC Health Services Research, 2022); 35% of Pacific peoples aged 65+ live with multimorbidity, more than double the European rate (MoH 2024); and Pacific peoples carry the highest unmet primary-care need of any ethnic group in Aotearoa (Health and Independence Report, 2024). The biological and structural causes are not mysterious. The unanswered question is whether a Pacific-designed, Pacific-delivered preventive model — at the scale of a properly powered trial — can move the needle on the early metabolic and mental-health markers that drive the gap.

We already have strong adjacent evidence. The Diabetes Prevention Program showed a 58% reduction in T2DM incidence with intensive lifestyle coaching in high-risk adults (DPP Research Group, NEJM 2002), and the OL@-OR@ HRC trial demonstrated that mHealth-supported lifestyle intervention can be acceptable and feasible for Māori and Pacific whānau (Verbiest et al., 2018). Te Tumu Waiora's evaluation showed that placing a health coach inside general practice "significantly increased access for three key groups not served well by traditional service approaches — young people, Māori and Pacific" (Te Pou / ProCare evaluation). What is missing from the New Zealand evidence base is a Pacific-led, telehealth-delivered, GP-plus-coach hybrid evaluated against usual care with adequate statistical power and cultural validity.

The timing is right for three reasons. First, Te Mana Ola explicitly calls for Pacific-led research generating Pacific-relevant evidence. Second, HRC's 2024 and 2025 Pacific Projects rounds prioritised exactly this kind of intervention research. Third, Connect Clinic's preventive programmes — Hauora Hinengaro (mental wellbeing), Mate Huka (diabetes prevention and reversal), and Whakaora Wawe (early intervention) — will have been operating for 12-18 months by the time recruitment begins, so the trial is evaluating a real-world service rather than a synthetic intervention built for a grant.

## 5. Proposed design

A pragmatic, mixed-methods cluster-randomised trial (with stepped-wedge fallback if cluster recruitment is slow), embedded within real Pacific primary care.

**Population.** 300-400 Pacific adults aged 25-65 with metabolic risk (HbA1c 41-49 mmol/mol, BMI ≥30, or first-degree T2DM family history) and/or mental wellbeing risk (Kessler-10 ≥12 or PHQ-9 ≥5), recruited via Pacific PHO networks (Alliance Health Plus, Pasifika Futures partner clinics), Pacific church networks (EFKT, PIPC, Catholic Pacific chaplaincies), and community fono. Stratified to ensure adequate Tongan, Samoan, Cook Islands Māori, Niuean, and Fijian representation. Māori participants of Pacific whakapapa included; pure Māori-identifying participants offered enrolment with kanohi-ki-te-kanohi engagement and Whakawhanaungatanga-led consent.

**Intervention arm.** 12-week structured Connect Clinic programme, delivered by telehealth on Elixir PMS:
- Weekly Pacific health coach sessions (lifestyle, behaviour change, mental wellbeing skills)
- Fortnightly Pacific GP review (clinical, prescribing, motivational interviewing)
- In-language consultations on request (Tongan, Samoan, Cook Islands Māori in-house; others via PMA-credentialled interpreters)
- Optional digital adjuncts: Mahino (medication literacy), Talanoa (language and cultural identity), Malo (Pacific CBT) — each opt-in, with analytics consent
- Stepped maintenance contact at 6 and 12 months

**Control arm.** Usual primary care via the participant's existing GP. No restriction on standard referrals; we measure what care actually occurs.

**Primary outcomes.** HbA1c change at 12 months; Kessler-10 (or PHQ-9 where culturally preferred) change at 12 months.

**Secondary outcomes.** Blood pressure, lipid panel, weight and waist circumference, medication adherence (Mahino app analytics with explicit consent + dispensing data), patient-reported wellbeing (WHO-5), cost-effectiveness (cost per QALY plus equity-weighted analysis), cultural fit measured by a Pacific-developed acceptability scale (we propose adapting the Fonofale-aligned items piloted in Pacific Trust Otago studies, Schluter et al.), and Whānau Ora-aligned process indicators.

**Qualitative strand.** Talanoa interviews with 30-40 participants and 10-15 whānau members at 6 and 12 months. Kanohi-ki-te-kanohi interviews with Māori participants. Thematic analysis through a Fonofale lens with Pacific researcher and kaumātua co-coding.

**Timeline.** 6 months set-up, ethics, registration; 18 months recruitment and intervention; 12 months follow-up overlap and analysis. Total 36 months matches the HRC ceiling.

## 6. Pacific-led governance

Governance is not a chapter at the end. It is the foundation.

- **Pacific Steering Group** chaired by a senior Pacific academic (we hope, you), with representation from each of the major Pacific communities recruited.
- **Kaumātua and Matua advisory** — at minimum one Tongan matāpule, one Samoan matai, one Cook Islands ariki representative, one Māori kaumātua of Pacific whakapapa for the Māori-Pacific intersection.
- **Methodology grounded in Pacific epistemologies.** Talanoa as method (Vaioleti, 2006); Fonofale (Pulotu-Endemann) as the wellbeing framework; Vā as the relational ethics standard for participant-researcher contact. Kaupapa Māori principles for the Māori cohort, Whakawhanaungatanga-led recruitment.
- **Data sovereignty.** Pacific data governance principles — data is held in Aotearoa, stewarded by the Pacific Steering Group, and any secondary use requires both academic IRB and Pacific community sign-off.

## 10. Why Connect Clinic

- **Operational delivery capability already running.** GP-plus-coach hybrid is live by mid-2026, on Elixir PMS, with Pacific clinicians delivering in Tongan, Samoan, and Cook Islands Māori today.
- **Pacific-GP-led.** Founder and clinical lead is a Tongan-NZ FRNZCGP-credentialled GP. Clinical decisions are Pacific decisions.
- **Workforce and language coverage in-house** rather than outsourced — talanoa happens in the patient's first language.
- **App team alongside the clinic.** Mahino (medication literacy), Talanoa (language learning), Malo (CBT in development) — these are research enablers (consented analytics, recruitment channels) and dissemination channels.
- **Programmes are already operating** by the time recruitment opens, so the trial is evaluating a real-world service, which strengthens external validity and the Te Whatu Ora rollout argument.
- **Independent of any single PHO**, so the host university is not boxed into one PHO's politics.
