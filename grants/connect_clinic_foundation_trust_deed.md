## 6. Charitable Purposes

### 6.1 Exclusively Charitable

The Foundation is established and shall be operated **exclusively** for Charitable Purposes within the meaning of section 5 of the Charities Act and the general law of New Zealand. No part of the income or property of the Foundation shall be used or applied for any non-charitable purpose.

### 6.2 The Charitable Purposes

The Charitable Purposes of the Foundation are:

**(a) Relief of poverty, sickness, and disadvantage** —
to relieve poverty, sickness, suffering, distress, and other forms of disadvantage among Pacific Peoples, Māori, and other under-served communities in Aotearoa New Zealand, in particular by improving access to high-quality, culturally responsive primary-care, preventive, and mental-health services.

**(b) Advancement of education** —
to advance education and public understanding in the fields of preventive medicine, lifestyle and behavioural change, mental wellbeing, chronic-disease prevention, hauora Māori, fakatangata mo'ui lelei (Pacific holistic wellbeing), and public health, including through the development and dissemination of educational resources, programmes, conferences, courses, and digital tools, and through scholarships and stipends for Pacific and Māori health workforce.

**(c) Advancement of health** —
to promote, protect, and advance the health of Pacific Peoples, Māori, and other communities in Aotearoa New Zealand, including by:
   (i) supporting early intervention, screening, and preventive-medicine programmes;
   (ii) promoting mental wellbeing and culturally appropriate mental-health support;
   (iii) facilitating telehealth and digital-health access for under-served, rural, and remote populations;
   (iv) reducing inequities in health outcomes between Pacific Peoples, Māori, and other New Zealanders.

**(d) Advancement of research** —
to undertake, fund, support, and disseminate research that is directed at any of the foregoing purposes, on the basis that:
   (i) the research is of a kind that adds to the sum of human knowledge in a field useful to the community;
   (ii) the results of any research are made available to the public on reasonable terms; and
   (iii) the research is conducted to a recognised standard of scholarship and ethics, including approval by an accredited Health and Disability Ethics Committee or equivalent where required.

**(e) Support for Pacific and Māori health workforce** —
to support the development, training, retention, and wellbeing of the Pacific and Māori health workforce in Aotearoa New Zealand, including through scholarships, mentoring programmes, professional development, conference attendance, supervision support, and grants.

**(f) Such other purposes** beneficial to the community in Aotearoa New Zealand as the Trustees may from time to time determine, **provided that** any such purpose is exclusively charitable in New Zealand law and consistent with the foregoing purposes.

### 6.3 Exclusion of Private Benefit

(a) No Charitable Purpose shall be construed in any way that confers a private pecuniary benefit on any individual.

(b) Notwithstanding clause 6.3(a):
   (i) a Trustee may be reimbursed for actual and reasonable out-of-pocket expenses properly incurred on behalf of the Foundation;
   (ii) a Trustee or Related Party may receive reasonable remuneration for goods or services provided to the Foundation, on arm's-length terms, only in accordance with clauses 8.7, 9, 10, and 12; and
   (iii) members of the Beneficiary Class may receive benefits in furtherance of the Charitable Purposes.

### 6.4 Politically Neutral

The Foundation shall not have, as a primary purpose, the advancement of any political party or candidate. The Foundation may, in furtherance of its Charitable Purposes, engage in advocacy and education on matters of public policy relevant to the health of the Beneficiary Class, consistent with the position established by the Supreme Court of New Zealand in *Re Greenpeace of New Zealand Inc* [2014] NZSC 105.

### 7.2 Secondary Beneficiary Class

## 8. Trustees

### 8.1 Minimum Number

The number of Trustees shall be not fewer than **three (3)** and not more than **seven (7)**.

### 8.2 Independent Trustees

At all times **at least two (2)** Trustees shall be Independent Trustees.

### 8.3 Appointment

(a) The first Trustees are those named in the Parties section above.
(b) Subsequent Trustees shall be appointed by resolution of a majority of the Trustees in office at the relevant time.
(c) The Trustees shall use reasonable endeavours to ensure that the composition of the trustee body reflects, where practicable, Pacific, Māori, clinical, governance, and financial expertise.

### 8.4 Term

Trustees are appointed for a term of three (3) years and are eligible for reappointment for further three-year terms. There is no maximum number of terms, but the Trustees should periodically review composition for renewal.

### 8.5 Retirement, Removal, and Vacation of Office

A Trustee shall cease to hold office on the occurrence of any of the following:
(a) written resignation delivered to the other Trustees;
(b) death or permanent incapacity;
(c) bankruptcy or being subject to a creditor compromise;
(d) being convicted of an offence punishable by imprisonment of two or more years (whether or not imprisonment was imposed);
(e) being disqualified from acting as an officer of a charitable entity under the Charities Act;
(f) being removed by unanimous written resolution of the other Trustees on the grounds of:
   (i) material breach of fiduciary duty;
   (ii) repeated unjustified absence from meetings;
   (iii) bringing the Foundation into disrepute; or
   (iv) any other ground that, in the reasonable opinion of the other Trustees, makes continued service unsuitable.

### 8.6 Vacancy

A vacancy in the office of Trustee shall be filled within ninety (90) days, **unless** the number of remaining Trustees is at least three (3) and at least two (2) of those remaining Trustees are Independent Trustees, in which case a vacancy may be left unfilled at the discretion of the remaining Trustees.

### 8.7 Conflicts of Interest

(a) Each Trustee shall, on appointment and at the start of each meeting, disclose all material direct and indirect interests in any matter to be considered by the Trustees, including any interest as a Related Party.

(b) A Trustee with a material conflict of interest in a matter shall:
   (i) declare the conflict in writing and on the meeting record;
   (ii) **not vote** on the matter;
   (iii) **not count** towards the quorum for that matter; and
   (iv) where the conflict is material, **leave the meeting** for the duration of the discussion and decision.

(c) A register of interests shall be maintained by the Trustees and reviewed at least annually.

### 8.8 Meetings, Quorum, and Decisions

(a) The Trustees shall meet not less than **four (4)** times each calendar year.
(b) Meetings may be held in person, by video conference, by telephone, or by any combination, provided each participating Trustee can hear and be heard by every other.
(c) The quorum for a meeting is a majority of the Trustees in office, of whom **at least one must be an Independent Trustee**.
(d) Decisions are made by simple majority of those present and entitled to vote, except where this Deed requires a higher threshold.
(e) The chairperson does not have a casting vote.
(f) A written resolution signed (including by electronic signature) by all Trustees in office is as valid as a resolution passed at a meeting.
(g) Minutes of all meetings and written resolutions shall be kept.

### 8.9 Chairperson

The Trustees shall appoint one of their number as chairperson for a renewable term not exceeding two years. The chairperson shall be an Independent Trustee unless all Trustees agree otherwise in writing.

## 9. Powers of Trustees

### 9.1 General Powers

In addition to the powers conferred by law (including the Trusts Act 2019), the Trustees have all the powers necessary or convenient for carrying out the Charitable Purposes, including the powers set out in this clause.

### 9.2 Specific Powers

The Trustees may, in furtherance of the Charitable Purposes:

(a) **Investment** — invest Trust Property in any form of investment that a prudent trustee would make, consistent with the Trusts Act 2019;

(b) **Receipt of funds** — receive and accept donations, gifts, bequests, devises, grants, sponsorships, koha, and other contributions, whether subject to conditions or not, **provided that** any conditions are consistent with the Charitable Purposes;

(c) **Grants and partnerships** — apply for, accept, and administer grants from public and philanthropic funders, and enter into partnerships, fiscal-sponsorship, or auspicing arrangements with Auspicee Partners;

(d) **Contracting** — enter into contracts of all kinds, including contracts with Connect Clinic Limited subject to clause 10;

(e) **Employment** — employ staff and engage contractors on terms the Trustees consider appropriate, including paying reasonable market-rate remuneration, KiwiSaver employer contributions, and complying with all employer obligations under New Zealand law;

(f) **Property** — purchase, lease, hold, manage, improve, mortgage, sell, or otherwise deal with real and personal property, including land and intellectual property;

(g) **Borrowing** — borrow money, on such terms (including security over Trust Property) as the Trustees consider prudent, **provided that** borrowings are used solely to advance the Charitable Purposes;

(h) **Bank accounts** — open, operate, and close bank accounts in the name of the Foundation;

(i) **Insurance** — take out insurance, including trustee indemnity insurance, professional indemnity insurance for clinical or research activities, public liability insurance, and cyber insurance;

(j) **Delegation** — delegate any power (other than the power to amend this Deed, to wind up the Foundation, or to authorise a Related-Party Transaction) to a Trustee, committee, employee, or contractor, with appropriate written terms of delegation and oversight;

(k) **Subsidiaries and ventures** — establish, hold shares in, or wind up subsidiary entities, **provided that** any such entity operates in furtherance of the Charitable Purposes;

(l) **Indemnify** — indemnify Trustees, employees, and contractors in accordance with clause 15;

(m) **Dispose of property** — sell, exchange, gift, or otherwise dispose of Trust Property in furtherance of the Charitable Purposes;

(n) **Professional advice** — engage and pay for legal, accounting, audit, tax, clinical, research, cultural, and other professional advice;

(o) **Anything incidental** — do anything incidental or conducive to the foregoing.

### 9.3 Arm's-Length Dealings with Related Parties

The Trustees shall not deal with any Related Party other than on arm's-length terms, with the prior written approval of a majority of Independent Trustees, in accordance with clause 10.

## 10. Related-Party Transactions

### 10.1 Connect Clinic Limited Services Agreement

(a) The Foundation may enter into a written services agreement (the **"CCL Services Agreement"**) with Connect Clinic Limited under which Connect Clinic Limited delivers clinical, administrative, technological, or other services on behalf of the Foundation in furtherance of the Charitable Purposes.

(b) The CCL Services Agreement must:
   (i) be in writing and signed by both parties;
   (ii) describe the services with reasonable specificity;
   (iii) provide for pricing on a **cost-plus-reasonable-overhead basis** or on **demonstrable market-rate terms**, whichever results in the lower cost to the Foundation, supported by a documented pricing methodology;
   (iv) include termination rights for the Foundation on no more than ninety (90) days' notice;
   (v) include audit rights for the Foundation over CCL's costs and overhead allocations relating to services delivered to the Foundation;
   (vi) prohibit any payment, distribution, or benefit flowing from the Foundation to Connect Clinic Limited shareholders other than at arm's-length consideration for services rendered.

### 10.2 Approval Process

(a) Any Related-Party Transaction (including the CCL Services Agreement and any amendment to it) requires:
   (i) prior written disclosure of all material terms and the conflict of interest;
   (ii) approval by **unanimous resolution of the Independent Trustees**, with no conflicted Trustee participating in the discussion or vote;
   (iii) documentation of the basis on which the Independent Trustees concluded that the transaction is on arm's-length terms and in the Foundation's best interests; and
   (iv) where the transaction value exceeds NZ$50,000 in any 12-month period, an **independent benchmark or market valuation** obtained at the Foundation's expense.

### 10.3 Annual Review

(a) Once per financial year, the Independent Trustees shall:
   (i) review the operation of the CCL Services Agreement and any other Related-Party Transactions;
   (ii) confirm that pricing remains on arm's-length terms;
   (iii) document the review in the meeting minutes; and
   (iv) record any changes required.

### 10.4 Public Disclosure

The Foundation shall disclose, in its annual return to Charities Services and in its annual financial statements:
(a) the existence of the CCL Services Agreement;
(b) the aggregate value of payments made to Related Parties during the financial year;
(c) the nature of services provided; and
(d) the basis on which arm's-length pricing was established.

## 12. Application of Income and Capital

### 12.1 Application Solely to Charitable Purposes

The income and capital of the Foundation shall be applied **exclusively** to the Charitable Purposes.

### 12.2 No Private Pecuniary Profit

No part of the income or property of the Foundation shall, directly or indirectly, be paid or transferred to any Trustee, Settlor, or Related Party by way of bonus, dividend, profit, or otherwise by way of private pecuniary profit, except as expressly permitted by this Deed.

### 12.3 Trustee Reimbursement and Remuneration

(a) A Trustee may be reimbursed for actual and reasonable out-of-pocket expenses properly incurred in the performance of trustee duties.

(b) A Trustee may receive reasonable remuneration for **professional services** rendered to the Foundation that are within the Trustee's professional competence (for example, clinical, research, legal, or accounting services), **only** if:
   (i) the engagement is approved in advance by unanimous resolution of the Independent Trustees, with the conflicted Trustee not present;
   (ii) the rate is not greater than a reasonable market rate for equivalent services;
   (iii) the engagement is documented in writing;
   (iv) the engagement is disclosed in the annual return and financial statements; and
   (v) the engagement complies with section CW 41 and CW 42 of the Income Tax Act 2007 and any other applicable law.

(c) A Trustee shall **not** be remunerated merely for serving as a Trustee. Honoraria for trustee service, if any, must be approved by Independent Trustees, must not exceed market norms for charitable trustees of comparable foundations, and shall be disclosed publicly.

### 12.4 Accumulation

The Trustees may accumulate income and capital for future application to the Charitable Purposes, subject to the prudent-investment duty under the Trusts Act 2019.

# Lawyer Review Brief

> Hand this list to your NZ-qualified solicitor along with the deed. These are the highest-value questions to spend their time on.

1. **Charitable purposes wording — registration robustness.** Will Charities Services accept the clause-6 list as exclusively charitable? In particular, are clauses 6.2(a)–(e) drafted tightly enough that the regulator will not bounce the application for being too broad or for blurring the line into political advocacy (post-*Greenpeace*)? Should any clause be re-cast as a "means" rather than a "purpose"?

2. **Dual-entity related-party robustness.** Is the related-party regime in clauses 8.7, 9.3, 10, and 12 strong enough to satisfy Charities Services, IRD, and a potential future audit that the Foundation is not being used to subsidise Connect Clinic Limited's commercial operations? Should we go further — e.g. require an independent benchmark for **every** transaction, lower the NZ$50k threshold, or appoint a fully independent committee outside the trustee body?

3. **Pricing methodology for the CCL Services Agreement.** What is the safest pricing model under NZ charity and tax law: cost-plus, market-rate, or the lower-of? How should "reasonable overhead" be defined? Is there case law or Charities Services guidance we should mirror?

4. **Donee organisation status (IT07).** Will the Foundation qualify for donee status from incorporation, or is there a waiting period? Are there specific clauses (winding-up, application of income, prohibition on private benefit) that need re-wording to satisfy IRD's requirements for donee approval and listing in Schedule 32 / under section LD 3?

5. **GST treatment.** Should the Foundation register for GST? What are the GST consequences of (a) receiving philanthropic grants; (b) receiving service revenue from contracts with PHOs, Te Whatu Ora, or ACC; and (c) paying Connect Clinic Limited under the CCL Services Agreement? Is there any zero-rating or GST exemption available?

6. **Trustee remuneration for clinical or research services.** Clause 12.3(b) permits a trustee (e.g. Dr Katoa as a GP) to be paid for professional services rendered to the Foundation. Is this safe under section CW 41/CW 42 of the Income Tax Act 2007 and Charities Services' guidance? Are there any structural risks (e.g. needing to record the engagement in the same way as a related-party transaction under clause 10)?

7. **Employer obligations.** If the Foundation employs staff (e.g. a research fellow, programme manager, or community health worker), what employment-law, ACC, KiwiSaver, payroll-tax, and PAYE obligations attach? Are there any charity-specific exemptions or considerations?

8. **Trustee liability and insurance.** What level of trustee indemnity / D&O insurance should be in place from day one? Are there specific NZ providers (Marsh, Aon, BMS) with charitable-trust-specific products we should compare? Does clause 15 sufficiently mirror section 73 of the Trusts Act 2019?

9. **Privacy and health-information handling.** If the Foundation runs research or programmes that handle identifiable health information, are there any deed-level provisions we should add (e.g. a data-governance committee, mandated HDEC review, an explicit Māori data-sovereignty clause)?

10. **Te Tiriti o Waitangi clause (7.4).** Is the wording strong enough, or should we expand it into a substantive obligation (e.g. a Māori advisory rōpū with binding referral rights for any Māori-facing programme)? What are funders likely to expect?

11. **Auspicee Partner / fiscal-sponsorship arrangements.** Are there any drafting changes needed to clause 9.2(c) to enable the Foundation to act as a fiscal sponsor for smaller Pacific or Māori community groups, without exposing it to liability or compromising its charitable status?

12. **Tier of PBE accounting and audit threshold.** At what point will the Foundation cross from Tier 4 to Tier 3 to Tier 2 reporting? At what revenue threshold does audit become mandatory? Should we pre-commit to higher reporting standards in the deed to signal governance maturity to funders?

# Filing Checklist (DIA Charities Services and IRD)

> Once the deed is finalised and signed, the registration pathway is approximately as follows.

## A. Pre-filing preparation

- [ ] Final deed signed by all three Trustees, witnessed, and dated.
- [ ] Certified copies of photo ID for each Trustee.
- [ ] NZBN application for the Foundation (free, via nzbn.govt.nz).
- [ ] Foundation IRD number (apply via IR1 or online once NZBN issued).
- [ ] Bank account opened in the name of "Connect Clinic Foundation" (most major NZ banks require: signed deed, NZBN, IRD number, Trustee ID and proof of address).
- [ ] Register of Trustees and register of interests started.
- [ ] Privacy policy, conflict-of-interest policy, and risk-management policy drafted (Charities Services likes to see these — not strictly required for registration but expected for governance maturity).

## B. Charities Services registration (DIA)

Filed online at **register.charities.govt.nz**.

- [ ] **Application form** for registration as a charitable entity (free).
- [ ] **Trust deed** (PDF, signed and dated).
- [ ] **Statement of charitable purposes** (a plain-English summary, even though it's also in the deed).
- [ ] **Officer details** for each Trustee (full name, DOB, address, role, occupation, certified ID).
- [ ] **Officer certifications**: each Trustee certifies they are not disqualified under section 16 of the Charities Act.
- [ ] **Activities and beneficiaries narrative** — concrete description of what the Foundation will do in its first 12 months and who will benefit.
- [ ] **Estimated income and expenditure** for the first financial year.
- [ ] **Rules and policies**: conflict-of-interest, related-party, financial controls.

**Expected timeline:** Charities Services typically processes applications in **8–12 weeks** from a complete submission. They will often come back with questions on charitable purpose wording, related-party arrangements, and beneficiary class. Build in time for one round of clarification.

## C. IRD registrations

- [ ] **IRD number application** (online via myIR or via paper IR596). Usually issued in 5–10 working days.
- [ ] **Donee organisation status** (IR1135 application, "Donee organisation – application for approval"). Required for donors to claim 33.33% rebate. Typically 4–8 weeks. Approval is automatic where the entity is registered as a charity and meets the donee criteria, but requires a separate application.
- [ ] **Income tax exemption** is automatic once registered as a charity under Charities Act, but confirm with the IRD that the entity is correctly coded as exempt (CW 41).
- [ ] **GST registration (IR360)** — only if turnover exceeds NZ$60,000 in any 12-month period, or if the Trustees elect to register voluntarily. Discuss with accountant.
- [ ] **Employer registration (IR334)** — required if the Foundation employs staff. Note: Francis's clinical entities currently use IR334 for employer registration; check that this is the correct current form (IRD changes form numbers from time to time).
- [ ] **PAYE / KiwiSaver setup** — if employing staff.

## D. Bank, insurance, and operational

- [ ] Open Foundation bank account.
- [ ] Set up Xero or equivalent with PBE-compliant chart of accounts.
- [ ] Trustee indemnity / D&O insurance quote (Marsh, Aon, BMS, Crombie Lockwood).
- [ ] Public liability and (if relevant) clinical / professional indemnity insurance.
- [ ] Cyber insurance (if Foundation will hold any health information).
- [ ] Logo, domain (connectclinicfoundation.org.nz), email, and basic website.
- [ ] CCL Services Agreement drafted, reviewed by Independent Trustees, signed.

## E. First 90 days post-registration

- [ ] First Trustees' meeting, confirming chairperson, approving policies, ratifying CCL Services Agreement.
- [ ] Notify Charities Services of any officer changes.
- [ ] First annual return is due **6 months after balance date** (if balance date is 31 March, first annual return due **30 September** of the following year).
- [ ] First financial statements prepared in line with PBE Tier 3 or Tier 4 standards.
