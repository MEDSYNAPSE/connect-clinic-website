# Connect Clinic Foundation — Charitable Trust Setup Checklist

**Goal:** Stand up a registered charitable trust within **30 days** to unlock philanthropic funding (Foundation North, J R McKenzie, Lottery Community, Tindall Foundation, Todd Foundation, COGS, etc.) that the Limited company cannot access.

**Working name:** Connect Clinic Foundation (Charitable Trust)

---

## 1. Step-by-step

### Day 0–7: Foundation
1. **Confirm three founding trustees** (see §2). Get signed consent-to-act forms and proof of ID for each.
2. **Draft the Trust Deed** (see §3 for purposes). Use a NZ charitable trust template (Community Law / Hugh Kettle / Parry Field have decent free skeletons) and adapt. Optional: spend ~$1,500 on a lawyer to review before signing — strongly recommended given the related-party arrangement with the Ltd.
3. **Choose a trust name** and check availability on the Companies Office register and the Charities Register. Connect Clinic Foundation is the working title — confirm no clashes.
4. **Settlor** — Francis settles the trust with a nominal sum (typically $10–$100). Record this in the Deed.
5. **Sign and date the Trust Deed** in front of a JP or solicitor (witnessing requirements). All trustees sign.

### Day 7–14: Registration
6. **Register as a Charitable Trust Board** with Companies Office under the Charitable Trusts Act 1957 — optional but recommended for legal personality (so the Trust can hold contracts and a bank account in its own name without all trustees being individually named). Fee: **~$103** online via the Charitable Trusts Board register.
   - *Alternative:* skip the Board registration and operate as an unincorporated trust — cheaper but trustees personally sign every contract. For a funded entity, Board registration is worth the $103.
7. **Apply to Charities Services (DIA)** for charitable registration via charities.govt.nz. Fee: **free** (DIA does not charge for registration; only the Companies Office Board fee applies). Submit:
   - Trust Deed
   - Officer details for all trustees (full legal name, DOB, address, declaration)
   - Statement of activities (what the trust will actually do — see §3)
   - Rules summary
   - Processing time: typically 4–6 weeks but can be expedited.
8. **Apply for an IRD number** for the Trust (separate from Francis's personal IRD and the Ltd's IRD). Free, online, ~5 working days.

### Day 14–30: Operationalise
9. **Apply for donee status** with IRD (form IR255 / now embedded in the charitable registration flow). Donee status lets donors claim the 33⅓% tax credit and is essentially mandatory for fundraising. Free.
10. **Open a dedicated bank account** in the Trust's name. ASB, BNZ, Westpac and Kiwibank all do not-for-profit accounts with low/no fees. Bring: certificate of incorporation (if Board), Trust Deed, IRD number, trustee IDs.
11. **Set up basic governance infrastructure**:
    - Trustee register
    - Conflict-of-interest register (critical — see §6)
    - Minute book (first meeting: appoint chair, secretary/treasurer, adopt policies)
    - Annual planner (AGM, annual return to Charities Services within 6 months of balance date)
12. **Draft three core policies before first grant application:**
    - Conflict of Interest policy (mandatory for the related-party arrangement)
    - Related-Party Transactions policy
    - Financial Delegations policy
13. **Sign the inter-entity Services Agreement** between the Foundation and Connect Clinic Limited (cost-plus-overhead pricing, approved by independent trustees with Francis recused — see §6).

## 2. Recommended initial trustees

Francis is one trustee. Need **at least two independent trustees** so the majority of the board can approve related-party transactions without Francis voting. Aim for **four trustees total** for a majority-independent board (3 independent + Francis).

Recommended profiles:

1. **Pacific community elder / matua** — ideally a respected Tongan or Samoan kaumātua/matāpule with standing in church or community networks (PIC, Catholic Pasifika, EFKS). Brings cultural authority and community trust. Honorary, not paid.
2. **Financial / governance trustee** — a chartered accountant (CA ANZ), former bank manager, or someone with NFP treasurer experience. Will chair the audit/finance subcommittee and is the pricing-fairness gatekeeper for the Ltd contract. Look at Pasifika finance professionals via PIPA (Pacific Island Professionals Association) or the Pasifika Medical Association network.
3. **Kaupapa Māori advocate / Māori health leader** — a Māori health professional, iwi health rep, or hauora provider leader. Critical because the Trust's purposes span both Pacific and Māori health equity, and a Māori voice at the table is non-negotiable for credibility with Te Aka Whai Ora successor bodies, Foundation North's mātauranga Māori lens, and J R McKenzie's Te Tiriti commitments.
4. *(Optional 5th)* **Clinical or public-health academic** — e.g. someone from AUT Pacific Health, Uni of Auckland Pacific Health, or a Pasifika public health specialist. Strengthens grant applications and clinical governance.

**Avoid:** Amberlie (spouse — disqualifies independence), Warren (employee/contractor of the Ltd — same), any clinician who refers patients to Connect Clinic.

## 3. Drafted Trust Deed Charitable Purposes

> The Trust is established exclusively for charitable purposes within the meaning of the Charities Act 2005, namely:
>
> **(a)** The advancement of health and the relief of disease, sickness, and human suffering, with a particular focus on Pacific peoples and Māori in Aotearoa New Zealand who experience inequitable health outcomes;
>
> **(b)** The advancement of education and health literacy among Pacific and Māori whānau, including in-language health education, lifestyle change support, and the prevention of long-term conditions such as type 2 diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and mental health conditions;
>
> **(c)** The relief of poverty and disadvantage by removing financial, geographic, linguistic, and cultural barriers that prevent Pacific and Māori whānau from accessing primary healthcare;
>
> **(d)** The promotion of culturally grounded models of care, including kaupapa Māori and kaupapa Pasifika frameworks (such as the Tongan Fonua model, Samoan Fonofale, Te Whare Tapa Whā, and Te Tumu Waiora);
>
> **(e)** The funding, commissioning, support, and operation of clinical, educational, and community-based programmes that further any of the above purposes, including (but not limited to) contracting with healthcare providers to deliver subsidised or free care to beneficiaries of the Trust;
>
> **(f)** Any other purpose beneficial to the community that is exclusively charitable under the laws of New Zealand.
>
> No part of the income or property of the Trust shall be paid to or for the personal benefit of any trustee, settlor, or related party, except for: (i) reasonable reimbursement of actual expenses; (ii) payment for goods or services rendered at no more than fair market value, approved by a majority of independent trustees with the related party recused.

**Key phrasing the lawyer must check:**
- "Particular focus on Pacific and Māori" — permitted under charitable law as relief of inequity (not unlawful discrimination) but should be drafted with care.
- The contracting clause (e) — explicitly authorises paying the Ltd for service delivery, removing ambiguity later.
- The no-private-benefit clause — this is what lets us register and is what we have to honour in the Ltd contract.

## 6. Dual-entity arrangement

**Connect Clinic Limited (the trading company)** delivers clinical services. Bills patients (private fees), insurers (Southern Cross, NIB), ACC, capitation contracts, sub-contracted Whānau Ora work, and any other revenue. Pays Francis, Warren, and other staff. Generates surplus, pays company tax.

**Connect Clinic Foundation (the charitable trust)** raises philanthropic capital — grants, donations, bequests, sponsored programmes. Cannot deliver services itself in any meaningful scale (no clinical infrastructure). Instead, **contracts the Ltd at cost-plus-overhead** to deliver subsidised care for beneficiaries who could not otherwise afford it (e.g. uninsured Pasifika kaumātua, Māori whānau on community services cards, refugees).

**Why this structure:**
- The Ltd is the right vehicle for revenue diversity, employment, scaling, and exit optionality.
- The Foundation is the only vehicle that unlocks philanthropic funding and the donor tax credit.
- The Foundation cannot just *be* the Ltd — they must be genuinely separate entities with separate governance, accounts, and decision-making.

**Related-party transaction governance — the bit auditors and Charities Services watch:**
1. **Pricing must be cost-plus-modest-overhead, not market rate.** Document the cost build-up: clinician time at award rates, Health Coach time at award rates, Elixir PMS share, Zoom share, admin overhead (cap at ~15%), no profit margin loaded on the Ltd's margin.
2. **Independent-trustee approval of every Services Agreement and any pricing variation.** Francis recuses from the vote, leaves the room, and the recusal is minuted.
3. **Annual review** — independent trustees re-attest each financial year that pricing remains fair and at-cost.
4. **Disclosed in both sets of financials** — the Foundation's annual return to Charities Services discloses the related-party transaction in full ($ amount, related party name, nature). The Ltd's accounts disclose the same.
5. **Conflict-of-Interest policy applied** — Francis declares his Ltd directorship/shareholding at every trustee meeting; standing item on the agenda.

## 7. Risks

1. **Charitable-purpose challenge** — Charities Services or a future review may ask: *"Is the Foundation actually doing public benefit, or is it a fundraising arm for a private business?"* If 100% of funds flow Ltd-ward, that's a real risk to registration.
   - **Mitigation:** the Foundation must do **some independent public-benefit activity** that is not mediated by the Ltd contract. Options:
     - Direct grant-making to other Pasifika/Māori health initiatives (even small grants, e.g. a $5k annual award to a Pasifika med student).
     - Free public health education resources (in-language T2DM/cardiovascular literacy materials, available to all, not just Connect Clinic patients).
     - Sponsorship of community health screening events (church-based, marae-based) where the Ltd is one of several providers.
     - Research grants or scholarships in Pacific/Māori primary care.
   - Aim for at least **15–20% of expenditure** on independent public-benefit activity by Year 2.

2. **Related-party scrutiny** — if pricing drifts above cost-plus, or if independent trustees rubber-stamp the Ltd contract, registration is at risk and there is reputational damage with funders. Foundation North in particular reads related-party notes carefully.
   - **Mitigation:** document everything, get the lawyer to draft the Services Agreement, get an external accountant to attest to the pricing methodology annually.

3. **Trustee independence under question** — if trustees are personal friends or all from Francis's church, "independence" looks thin. Funders pattern-match on this.
   - **Mitigation:** at least one trustee with no prior personal relationship to Francis. The financial governance trustee is the natural fit.

4. **Donor confusion** — donors may not understand why their gift to the Foundation pays the Ltd. Comms must be crystal clear about the cost-recovery model and the public benefit delivered.

5. **Tax position of the Ltd** — payments received from the Foundation are taxable revenue to the Ltd (no GST exemption automatically, check with accountant — health services are GST-exempt but admin fees may not be).
